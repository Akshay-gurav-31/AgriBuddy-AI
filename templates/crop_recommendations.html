Soil Type Recommendations

Clay Soil

Heavy, nutrient-rich soil that retains water well.

  • Best Crops: Rice, wheat, barley, cabbage
  • Improvement: Add sand and organic matter
  • Drainage: Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging

Sandy Soil

Light, well-draining soil that warms quickly.

  • Best Crops: Carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, peppers
  • Improvement: Add compost and clay
  • Watering: Requires frequent watering

Loamy Soil

Ideal soil with balanced sand, silt, and clay.

  • Best Crops: Most vegetables, fruits, and grains
  • Maintenance: Regular organic matter additions
  • Drainage: Excellent natural drainage

Seasonal Crop Guide

Kharif Season (June-October)

Monsoon season crops

  • Rice, maize, sorghum, pearl millet
  • Soybean, groundnut, cotton
  • Pulses: Black gram, green gram
  • Vegetables: Cucumber, bitter gourd

Rabi Season (October-March)

Winter season crops

  • Wheat, barley, oats
  • Peas, mustard, chickpeas
  • Vegetables: Potato, onion, carrot
  • Oilseeds: Rapeseed, linseed

Zaid Season (March-June)

Summer season crops

  • Watermelon, muskmelon, cucumber
  • Bitter gourd, bottle gourd
  • Fodder crops: Berseem, cowpea
  • Early planting of some rabi crops

Crop Rotation Benefits

1

Legumes

Start with nitrogen-fixing crops like beans or peas to enrich soil.

2

Leafy Vegetables

Follow with nitrogen-loving crops like lettuce or spinach.

3

Fruiting Crops

Plant heavy feeders like tomatoes or peppers next.

4

Root Crops

End with light feeders like carrots or radishes to restore balance.
